Beyond MMLU: benchmarks that actually differentiate models

Every frontier model scores 88–94% on MMLU, so it no longer differentiates them. These three benchmarks still do — revealing real gaps in coding, science, and expert-level reasoning.i

What this means

"Saturated" means every frontier model clusters at 88–94% on MMLU — the scores are so close that you can't tell which model is actually better.

The three tabs above measure what still differentiates models:

  • SWE-bench — can the model fix real GitHub bugs?
  • GPQA Diamond — can it answer PhD-level science questions?
  • HLE — can it answer the hardest expert-level questions across 100+ subjects?

On these, models are not bunched at the top. Top HLE score: 64.7%. Bottom: 2.7%. That spread is why these benchmarks still matter.

What is SWE-bench?

Software Engineering Benchmark — real GitHub issues from popular Python repos (Django, Flask, scikit-learn). The model must read the issue, navigate a large codebase, find the bug, and write a patch that passes all tests. No hand-holding. No explicit pointer to the broken file.

This is the benchmark that matters for developers. It measures whether a model can do actual engineering work — not just answer questions about it.

What is GPQA Diamond?

Graduate-Level Google-Proof Q&A — 198 multiple-choice questions in biology, chemistry, and physics written by PhD experts. They're specifically designed so that skilled non-experts can't Google the answer, even with 30 minutes and unrestricted web access.

Tests genuine scientific reasoning, not recall. The questions require you to think like a domain expert. Human PhD specialists score ~65%.

What is MMLU?

Massive Multitask Language Understanding — 15,908 multiple-choice questions across 57 subjects, from high school biology to professional law to college physics. Models are shown 5 example Q&A pairs then must answer without any specialized training on those questions.

Once the gold standard for comparing models. Now saturated — frontier models all cluster at 88–94%, making it useless as a differentiator. The field has moved on.

What is Humanity's Last Exam?

HLE was created by the Center for AI Safety + Scale AI specifically because MMLU and GPQA were getting too easy. It has 2,500 graduate-level questions across 100+ subjects — math, chemistry, history, law, philosophy — crowdsourced from ~1,000 PhD experts across 50 countries. Questions are specifically designed to be un-Googleable.

The chart above shows the steepest climb in benchmark history: from ~3% in Jan 2025 to 64.7% for Claude Mythos (limited access) — but human experts still score ~90%, and the gap remains vast.

What "calibration error" means

Calibration = how well a model's stated confidence matches how often it's actually right.

A well-calibrated model that says "I'm 90% sure" should be right ~90% of the time.

What's happening on HLE: models only score ~40% accuracy — but when they're wrong, they still claim 80–95% certainty. They don't know when they don't know.

Why this matters: you can't tell from the model's tone whether to trust the answer. It'll state a made-up citation, wrong formula, or bad legal reasoning with the same confident tone as a correct answer.
