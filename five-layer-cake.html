Where these numbers come from

Most of what's on this page is projection or estimate — not settled fact. Different analysts give different numbers depending on what they count and how. Here's the receipt for each major claim, so you can argue with the methodology, not the messenger.

~50% of US GDP growth in 2026 from AI investment.
Renaissance Macro Research (Neil Dutta) tracking AI capex's contribution to US growth — widely reported through Bloomberg, FT, and Joe Weisenthal's Odd Lots coverage. Goldman Sachs and BofA have published similar (slightly lower) estimates.
$700B Magnificent 7 combined 2026 capex.
High-end projection from Bloomberg Intelligence and Morgan Stanley aggregating company guidance + analyst models. Conservative estimates land closer to $500–600B; the $700B figure assumes hyperscaler capex grows ~40% YoY and Apple/Tesla/Nvidia hold steady. 2025 confirmed hyperscaler capex (AMZN+GOOGL+META+MSFT) is ~$330B.
AI is the largest new electricity demand in the developed world.
IEA Electricity 2024 report on data center demand growth, plus Goldman Sachs "Generational Growth: AI, Data Centers and the Coming US Power Demand Surge" (April 2024).
Microsoft restarting Three Mile Island.
Constellation Energy press release, Sept 20, 2024. 20-year PPA between Microsoft and the restarted Unit 1 reactor.
Foundation model training costs.
Dario Amodei (Anthropic CEO) on training runs trending toward $1B+ — In Good Company podcast (2024) and his "Machines of Loving Grace" essay. GPT-4 was reported at ~$100M; Gemini Ultra estimated at ~$190M (Stanford AI Index 2024).
"More milestones in 6 years than the prior 70 combined."
Pulled directly from the event counts in this workshop's AI Timeline — 26 events 1950–2019, 81+ events 2020–present. Methodology: notable model releases, infrastructure shifts, and regulatory milestones tracked in the timeline dataset.
Nvidia / TSMC dominance.
Nvidia market cap surpassed $4T in 2025 (real-time via any market tracker). TSMC fabricates an estimated 90%+ of advanced-node (≤7nm) chips globally — Boston Consulting Group / SIA semiconductor reports.

Every number here was current as of May 2026. Projections shift quarterly. The point of this page isn't the exact figures — it's the shape of what's happening: five layers, all moving at once, with capital allocation unlike anything since the build-out of the railroads or electric grid.

The story in one line
Every dollar you spend on Claude — or any AI tool — touches all five layers. That's why the acceleration is exponential, not linear: every layer makes the layer above it faster, cheaper, and more capable, at the same time.
